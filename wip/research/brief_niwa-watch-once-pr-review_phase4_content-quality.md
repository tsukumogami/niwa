# Content Quality Review

**Verdict:** PASS

The revised BRIEF states a genuine two-part problem, keeps its outcome anchored to developer experience and safety properties, and its four journeys and in/out boundaries hold up; the new act-boundary and three scope refinements land at scope altitude rather than descending into design detail.

## Issues Found
1. Outcome third paragraph flirts with mechanism: The clauses "run outside the sandbox" and "holding a credential scoped to nothing beyond posting that one review" are the most design-leaning text in the User Outcome. They stay acceptable because each is subordinated to an experience/safety claim ("That is what lets 'post it or discard' be both a real one-gesture action and a safe one -- the act boundary the whole feature turns on"), so the paragraph reads as *why the safe one-gesture outcome holds* rather than a feature list. It is one edit from tipping over. Suggested fix: if tightened, lead with the property (the developer never re-exposes the read agent) and let the credential-scoping detail stay implicit, since the Open Question already defers how the posting credential is provisioned.
2. The act-boundary invariant is restated three times in near-identical wording: It appears in the User Outcome third paragraph, the IN "post-on-approval" bullet, and the OUT "posting by un-caging" bullet. This is structurally defensible (outcome frames the experience, IN scopes the requirement, OUT rejects the anti-goal), but the phrasing "separate trusted step / credential scoped to posting alone / read agent never posts" is copy-paste close across all three. Suggested fix: vary the framing per section so each earns its place rather than reading as duplication.

## Suggested Improvements
1. Journey 3 and the "adversarial test as part of done" IN bullet describe the same denial mechanics (network egress, push, arbitrary posting denied at the tool/OS layer). A one-clause cross-reference (the journey is the property the test asserts) would make the coupling intentional rather than redundant.
2. The OUT entry "Closing the sandbox's residual caveats" (Windows gap, non-TLS-terminating egress proxy, domain-fronting seam) is unusually specific for a brief and edges toward design detail. It passes as an honest known-risk disclosure rather than a strawman, but consider whether the domain-fronting seam belongs in the DESIGN threat model instead of the brief's boundary.

## Summary
The Problem Statement frames two coupled problems -- proactive staging as a convenience, and the untrusted-content remote-execution risk that forces containment to ship with it -- without smuggling a solution. The four journeys each name a user, trigger, and outcome shape and are cleanly distinct (happy path with post-on-approval, idempotent re-run, hostile-PR containment, team-only exclusion), and the Scope Boundary draws real lines: the new IN items (GitHub narrowing, trusted post step, fail-closed containment, per-run staging bound) stay at requirement altitude and the OUT anti-goal ("never un-cage the review agent to post") is a genuinely rejected alternative, not a deferral. Open Questions defer semantics to the PRD and explicitly disclaim blocking the framing; the mechanism-leaning outcome clause and the triple restatement are polish, not defects.
